# Teaching Visual Policy

Neutral Yb Atlas does not use generated laboratory photographs or decorative atomic imagery as teaching evidence. A visual belongs in the learning path only when it exposes a physical dependency, an experimental interface or an acceptance decision more clearly than prose alone.

## Required Anatomy

Every core teaching visual must provide:

1. one direct title, without a learning-objective box;
2. named physical objects, internal structures or measured states;
3. a realistic apparatus view plus the cutaways or local schematics needed to expose hidden information;
4. at least one numerical anchor, formula or explicit variable set;
5. an adjustment order or operation that an experimentalist can execute;
6. the instrument, curve, image or record used to observe the result;
7. explicit provenance as an atomic constant, literature apparatus example, engineering starting point or local calibration;
8. concise Chinese first-pass explanation in the image plus non-repetitive bilingual expansion in HTML.

## Implemented Visual Families

- first-principles deduction tree: postulates to logical-cycle evidence;
- Yb energy tutor: transition, role, hardware and acceptance evidence;
- Rydberg blockade CZ tutor: preparation, blockade, conditional phase and return;
- apparatus pipeline: oven, UHV, MOT, tweezers, coherent control, Rydberg gate, imaging and QEC feedback;
- research ecosystem: atomic data, theory, hardware, control, experiment, QEC and supply chain;
- contextual reference figures: complete maps and model outputs shown only after the relevant teaching structure.

## Generated-Image Boundary

Generated apparatus images are direct experimental explainers, not decorative laboratory substitutes or generic course slides. They combine photoreal apparatus with selected cutaways, local diagrams and measured-data insets. Raster text provides concrete numbers, actions and observables; HTML provides source-aware depth, formulas and bilingual expansion without repeating the same short explanation.
